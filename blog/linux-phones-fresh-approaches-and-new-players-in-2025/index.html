Jolla Community Phone

Jolla, the company behind Sailfish OS, has announced a new community-focused device. This continues their mission to provide an alternative mobile operating system that respects user privacy while offering a polished mobile experience.

Sailfish OS Features


  • Gesture-based navigation

  • Android app compatibility

  • Strong European privacy focus

  • Active community development

    • SHIFTphone

    SHIFTphone stands out as an impressive Linux-compatible smartphone, offering users a sustainable and repairable option with strong Linux support.

    Key Features


  • Modular, repairable design

  • Fair trade and sustainable manufacturing

  • Multiple OS options including Linux distributions

  • Long-term support commitment

    • The Current Linux Phone Landscape

    Established Players

  • Purism Librem 5: The flagship open-hardware Linux phone

  • Pine64 PinePhone/PinePhone Pro: Affordable, community-driven devices

  • Fairphone: Sustainable phones with Linux port support

    • New Entrants

  • Mecha Comet: New approach to mobile Linux

  • Liberux/Nexx: Emerging alternatives

  • FLX1: Fresh take on the Linux phone concept

  • FuriPhone: Another contender in the space

    • Market Trends in 2025

  • Increased focus on privacy and user control

  • Growing demand for sustainable, repairable devices

  • More distributions supporting mobile form factors

  • Better convergence (phone-to-desktop) capabilities

  • Improved app ecosystem

    • Updates and Corrections

    The video also addresses updates and corrections to previous Linux phone coverage, ensuring accurate information about the current state of the market.

    Why Linux Phones Matter

  • Privacy: No Google or Apple tracking

  • Longevity: Software support not tied to manufacturer whims

  • Freedom: Full control over your device

  • Sustainability: Repair and extend device lifespan

  • Convergence: One device for mobile and desktop computing
